How does a parallax work?

A parallax is a visual feature that gives the impression that the page when scrolled, elements move at different speeds relative to one another.

Their implementation is effective for images with depth of field.

In this example, we assign a container inside the body tag that acts as our scroll container.

Limitations to this approach could be accessibility: there are now two scrollbars: one for the parallax wrapper context and the default one on body tag; the disappearing text wasn't attempted in this solution.
